"What... what did you say?"

It's close to six o'clock on a weekday afternoon, and the bar is starting to get noisy with the after-work crowd. It's entirely possible I misheard that last part.

"Altoids! I find the spearmint works a little better overall, but recently I've started switching flavors depending on the situation."

I've worked with James---"Jim" to disambiguate from the other James in QA---for a little over two years and I'm used to this dance now. He gets a kind of tunnel vison in his excitemtnt about whatever shiny new thing has captured his attention. It's usually pretty easy to shake him out of it.

"No, Jim, the part before that."

He looks at me for a moment, puzzled, before pushing his beer aside. "Here, let me show you." He reaches underneath the table and produces his Timbuk2 messenger bag. He plops it on the table, right in the wet spot his drink had left behind. He unzips the outermost pouch and pulls out a small green and white box. He lays it on top of the bag, obviously wanting me to see this as clearly as possible.

"Regular Altoids, right? You take about four of them, maybe five." He opens the lid and traps the requisite number of small white mints in his fingertips. He pops them all into his mouth. "This is the trick; you gotta chew first." At least two tiny shards fly out of his mouth as he speaks the words. Three more slow and deliberate chomps, then he pauses. "Mmm." He opens the main pouch of the bag and roots around for a few seconds before finding the star of this particular show.

My eyes barely have enough time to resolve the object in the dismal light at this end of the bar before it's in his mouth. He's chewing the full concoction now---mouth closed, thank God. He's looking at me with a kind of confident smugness I haven't seen since I bet my buddy at Guitar Center that he couldn't play "Everlong" unprompted. (I lost.)

Jim is making a show of this. Each jaw movement deliberate. Precise. He does not break eye contact with me, though I desperately want to break it with him. I can't though. It's like a significant piece of my brain has just completely shut off. He swallows one final time, smacks his lips, then opens his mouth wide like a child proving that they finished their vegetables and have earned a delicious dessert. "Easy peasy, no problem."

"That was..." I'm just saying words without thinking. Filling the empty space.

"A cat turd," he says, finishing my sentence.

A beat.

"You just ate a cat turd." It's all I can do in the moment to restate the facts as I understand them, although the sense of alarm is definitely carrying in my voice.

"Yup, and it didn't taste bad at all. The Altoids mask it _completely._ Watch, I'll do another one..." My eyes widen in dread as I shake my head weakly. I didn't want to see that the first time; I sure as shit don't want to see it again. "No, that's alright."
