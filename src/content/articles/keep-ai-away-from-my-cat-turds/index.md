"What... what did you say?"

It's close to six o'clock on a weekday afternoon, and the bar is starting to get noisy with the after-work crowd. It's entirely possible I misheard that last part.

"Altoids! I find the spearmint works a little better overall, but recently I've started switching flavors depending on the situation."

I've worked with James---"Jim" to disambiguate from the other James in QA---for a little over two years and I'm used to this dance now. He gets a kind of tunnel vision in his excitement about whatever shiny new thing has captured his attention. It's usually pretty easy to shake him out of it.

"No, Jim, the part before that."

He looks at me for a moment, puzzled, before pushing his beer aside. "Here, let me show you." He reaches underneath the table and produces his Timbuk2 messenger bag. He plops it down in the wet spot his drink had left behind. He unzips the outermost pouch and pulls out a small green and white box. He lays it on top of the bag, obviously intending for me to see this as clearly as possible.

"Regular Altoids, right? You take about four of them, maybe five." He opens the lid and traps the requisite number of small white mints in his fingertips. He pops them all into his mouth. "This is the trick; you gotta chew first." At least two tiny shards fly out of his mouth as he speaks these words. It is like listening to a slow K-turn executed on a road made of gravel and seashells. Three more slow and deliberate chomps, then he pauses. "Mmm." He opens the main pouch of the bag and roots around for a few seconds before finding the star of this particular show.

My eyes barely have enough time to resolve the object in the dismal light at this end of the bar before it's in his mouth. He's chewing the full concoction now---mouth closed, thank God. He's looking at me with a kind of confident smugness I haven't seen since I bet my buddy at Guitar Center that he couldn't play "Everlong" spontaneously from memory. (I lost that bet.)

Jim is making a show of this. Each jaw movement deliberate. Precise. He does not break eye contact with me, though I desperately want to break it with him. I can't though. It's like a significant piece of my brain has just completely shut off. He swallows one final time, smacks his lips, then opens his mouth wide like a child proving that they finished their vegetables and have earned a delicious dessert. "Easy peasy, no problem."

"That was..." I'm just saying words without thinking. Filling the empty space.

"A cat turd," he says, finishing my sentence.

A beat.

"You just ate a cat turd." It's all I can do in the moment to restate the facts as I understand them, although the sense of alarm is definitely carrying in my voice.

"Yup, and it didn't taste bad at all. The Altoids mask it _completely._ Watch, I'll do another one..." My eyes widen in dread as I shake my head weakly. I didn't want to see that the first time; I sure as shit don't want to see it again. "No, that's alright."

He reaches across his bag and grabs his glass, tips it toward me in a silent toast, and takes a long swill. Evidently there is something that needs to be washed down. He lets out a relaxed sigh as he places the almost-empty glass back on the table. I glance down briefly at the chicken wings and celery sticks I had been eating a few minutes earlier. I now have no appetite to ever resume eating that.

He picks the small box of mints up and gives it a little shake in front of my face. "Altoids. I'm not exaggerating when I say these have completely changed the way I work." He places them back into his bag and begins to zip up the pouch. He stops abruptly and looks up at me again. "Would you like to try?"

"No, Jim, I don't want your cat turds."

I don't want _your_ cat turds. Why did I say it like that? I don't want _anybody's_ cat turds!

_...right?_

"Completely changed the way I work," he repeats mechanically, putting his bag back down on the empty seat to his left. "I used to spend so much time on the cat turds, psyching myself up, trying strategies that didn't work, all the cleanup... That's all changed now. I can never go back to the old way."

"I just... I mean..." My brain has started working again, at least a little, and has generated so many questions that I'm having difficulty choosing one to ask. "How long have you been eating cat turds?" That's a fine question for this moment, I suppose.

"What do you mean? I've always ate cat turds. Since I was a kid in school, on through college, in all my jobs... They keep giving me cat turds and I have to eat them, otherwise they start to pile up and then things really get unpleasant." His face turns slightly more serious as he reads my expression, and he tilts his head inquisitively. "You eat cat turds too, yeah?" I choose not to answer that question. He continues regardless. "We all do. We have to do it."

---

I arrive at home and shut the door behind me. Sunset was over an hour ago and it's nearly pitch black in the hallway. I fumble around for the light switch an turn it on, kick my shoes off next to the doorway, and hang my bag on its hook in the coat closet. Something grabs my attention, just above eye level, slightly overhanging the edge of the top shelf. I slide it off the shelf and carry it into the kitchen. I sit down at the table and inspect it.

This object is a round metal cookie tin about twelve inches in diameter. It is a deep red with a repeating pattern of white snowflakes, and clearly once held winter holiday--themed treats. I repurposed it many years ago to hold my favorite treats: mostly different shapes and styles of chocolate candies that I have collected over the years. I remove the lid and set it aside. I survey the contents, a sea of differently-shaped naked chocolate morsels. I don't remember why I chose to remove all the foil and paper wrapping before putting these in here. Everything looks vaguely the same---I can't readily spot any differences between milk chocolate and dark, or those filled with caramel versus cream.

One particular piece near the edge catches my eye, and I carefully select it for inspection. It's not a very pleasing color or shape. I roll it around in my fingers. There's a hair on it. I hold it up to my nose and take a whiff, hoping to detect the aroma of the cacao. Try as I might, I can't smell it.

Come to think of it, I can't remember the last time I smelled anything.
